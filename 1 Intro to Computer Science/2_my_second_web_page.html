Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace, whose claim to fame is being one of the first computer programmers, was born December 10, 1815 in London. She was the only legimate child of Byron who shortly divorced her mother. Byron died when Lovelace was 8 years old and her mother strongly encouraged her interest in math and logical reasoning because she didn't want her to be like her father who was thought to have been insane.

Lovelace was fascinated by the functionality of the human brain and strived to create a mathematical model to describe it. Altough she never reached that goal, she did leave a legacy in the math field nonetheless. Captivated by Babbage's difference engine, she aided him in his design of a theoretical analytical engine that went beyond pure math calculations. She created the first algorithm designed for a computer which would compute Bernoulli numbers. Unfortunately, the engine was never finished, but it's speculated her algorithm would've worked if it had been.

The analytical engine provided significant implications to the mathematical and programming community, as it proved computers were more than just number crunching machines. It was the first stepping stone towards the modern computer that is much more than the simple calculator computers were at the time. This amazing engine brought the realization that anything could be converted to numbers, even music.
